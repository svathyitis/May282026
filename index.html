Bhagavad Gita · Ch. 9 · Arjuna & Krishna
अनित्यमसुखं लोकमिमं
प्राप्य भजस्व माम्
anityam asukhaṁ lokam imaṁ prāpya bhajasva mām
One shloka to return to centre
The knowledge line
🍂
Anityam
This world is impermanent.
We live as though it will last forever — as though we will live forever. Anityam is the reminder: everything changes, everything passes.
🫧
Asukham
Lasting happiness cannot be found here.
Not "zero joy" — but no permanent bliss. Joy → attachment → fear of loss → sorrow. That cycle is Asukham.
🌍
Lokam Imam Prāpya
Having come into this world / understood this truth —
The pivot. Not a rejection of life, but a clarity about its nature.
🕊️
Bhajasva Mām
Remember, seek, abide in the Divine.
"Remember me" — not a personality. The Divine reality that is permanent, blissful, the source of all.
"This world is temporary and incapable of giving permanent joy — therefore turn your mind toward the Divine."
Guruji's key — when miserable, say:
"Oh! I forgot — Anityam Asukham Lokam Imam Prāpya Bhajasva Mām"

And you immediately return to centre.

Going deeper — the full picture
But the world also gives joy?
Yes — and Krishna celebrates it. The Gita is full of beauty, love, music, friendship, dharma, rasa. Even God came to the world to experience joy. The point of Asukham is subtler: worldly joy is temporary, mixed, and dependent — it cannot be the source of lasting bliss.
Adjacent verse — the wise person's relationship to pleasure
ये हि संस्पर्शजा भोगा दुःखयोनय एव ते
आद्यन्तवन्तः कौन्तेय न तेषु रमते बुधः
ye hi saṁsparśajā bhogā duḥkha-yonaya eva te
ādy-antavantaḥ kaunteya na teṣu ramate budhaḥ
Pleasures born of the senses have a beginning and an end — and therefore bring dissatisfaction. The wise enjoy them without becoming trapped by them.
The tension — and how Vedanta holds both
🌊
Joy arrives. It is real. Receive it fully.
🍃
Joy changes. It passes. Don't cling.
🎭
Krishna's lila — play, dance, love, friendship — is full participation in life.
🔑
Vedanta doesn't reject the world. It warns against expecting the finite to give infinite satisfaction.
The reconciliation
Enjoy the world. Don't depend on it for permanent bliss.
or in Vedantic language:
Participate fully, possess lightly.

Why this shloka centres you
When misery arises, it is almost always because we expected permanence from the impermanent, or bliss from the world. The shloka names exactly that error — and in naming it, dissolves it. The remembering is the return.
